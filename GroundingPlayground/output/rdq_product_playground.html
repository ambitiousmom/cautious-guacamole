RDQ Perspective

RDQ (Rich Data Quorum) measures grounding data quality across 3 layers: Coverage, Richness, and Sufficiency. Here's where we stand:

Key Insights (State of Things)

1
Layer 1 (Coverage) is strong ‚Äî we have broad entity coverage
Our index covers most entities with basic attributes: name, address, hours, ratings, photos, and reviews. This is foundational grounding data.
2
Layer 2 (Richness) has gaps ‚Äî structured amenities and facets are sparse
We lack structured boolean flags for amenities (outdoor seating, parking, WiFi) and occasion signals (romantic, kid-friendly). This data exists in reviews but isn't extracted.
3
6 POCs are actively working on Richness ‚Äî but encumbrance blocks production
AI Enrichment, Facet Extraction, and other POCs can generate rich content. However, legal/policy classification for grounding use hasn't been finalized.
4
Layer 3 (Sufficiency) is unmeasured ‚Äî unclear if Copilot uses available data
Even where we have grounding-ready data, we don't yet measure whether Copilot retrieves and uses it effectively. This is a measurement gap.

Encumbrance Reality

What data can actually be used for Copilot grounding?
Encumbrance Level Can Ground? Example Sources Impact
Factual Yes Basic facts, hours, address Full RDQ contribution
RequiresAttribution With citation WrapStar, Schema.org extracts Partial ‚Äî needs citation UX
Restricted No Licensed feeds with restrictions Zero grounding value
AI-Generated (TBD) ? AI Enrichment POC outputs Blocked until policy decision
Key Insight
We may have high RDQ Layer 1 (coverage) but low "grounding-ready" coverage because much of our rich data has encumbrance restrictions. This is a critical blocker for competitive parity.

Competitive Insights

Where competitors have an advantage and why
1
Gemini has structured boolean amenities ‚Äî we infer from reviews
Google Maps provides direct flags like "Outdoor seating: true". Bing must parse reviews to answer, leading to lower confidence and occasional errors.
2
Gemini has "Most Ordered" and "Tips" ‚Äî we don't surface menu insights
Google shows crowd-sourced menu recommendations. For "What should I order at X?" queries, Gemini wins with specific dish suggestions.
3
Gemini has Occasion signals (Romantic, Groups, etc.) ‚Äî we have raw review text
"Is this good for a romantic dinner?" ‚Äî Gemini has structured occasion tags. We must synthesize from reviews, which is less reliable.
4
Hours and basic info are at parity
For hours, ratings, address, and phone ‚Äî we match or exceed competitors. This is not a gap area.

Potential Experiments

Prioritized ideas based on identified gaps
EXP-01
Structured Boolean Extraction from Reviews
Medium
Hypothesis: We can match Google's structured amenity flags by extracting booleans from review text with high confidence
Gap Addressed: Gemini has 'Outdoor seating: true' - we infer from reviews
Approach: Extend Facet Extraction POC to output boolean flags when confidence > 0.9
Success Metric: 90% precision on amenity flags vs. Google ground truth
L2 - Direct facet parity Encumbrance: Low - derived from our review data
EXP-02
AI-Generated 'Tips' and 'Most Ordered'
Low - prompt engineering
Hypothesis: LLM can synthesize review highlights into Gemini-style 'Tips' and 'Most Ordered' insights
Gap Addressed: Gemini shows 'Most Ordered: Tasting Menu' - we don't surface this
Approach: Add prompts to AI Enrichment for menu/tip extraction
Success Metric: User preference for AI-tips vs. no tips in A/B test
L2 - Rich insights Encumbrance: Medium - AI-generated content policy unclear
EXP-03
Review Summarization for Vibe/Occasion
Medium
Hypothesis: We can answer 'Is this good for X?' queries by summarizing relevant reviews
Gap Addressed: Gemini has 'Romantic: true', 'Good for groups: true'
Approach: Cluster reviews by occasion/vibe, generate structured signals
Success Metric: Match human labels for occasion suitability
L2 - Occasion facets Encumbrance: Low - synthesis from multiple reviews
EXP-04
Grounding-Ready Encumbrance Classification
High - legal/policy work
Hypothesis: We can classify which content is safe for Copilot grounding
Gap Addressed: We have rich data but unclear what's grounding-ready
Approach: Audit top providers for encumbrance, create whitelist
Success Metric: Clear grounding policy per data source
L1/L2/L3 - Unlocks existing data for grounding Encumbrance: N/A - this IS the encumbrance work
EXP-05
Competitive Facet Parity Tracking
Medium
Hypothesis: Automated tracking of facet gaps vs. competitors enables prioritization
Gap Addressed: Manual competitor analysis doesn't scale
Approach: Build pipeline: sample queries ‚Üí competitor responses ‚Üí facet extraction ‚Üí gap report
Success Metric: Weekly facet parity dashboard
L2 - Measurement Encumbrance: Low - competitive intelligence
EXP-06
SLM Cost Reduction for Enrichment
In Progress (penglinhuang)
Hypothesis: Gemma-3 can produce 80% of GPT-5 quality at 10% of cost
Gap Addressed: AI Enrichment too expensive to scale to all entities
Approach: A/B test SLM vs. GPT-5 outputs on quality metrics
Success Metric: Quality parity at lower cost
L2 - Scale Encumbrance: Same as GPT-5
